# Efficient-affective-analysis
Trade-offs of Large Audio Language for Emotion Recognition
Models for Emotion Recognition Large Language Models excel in many tasks, particularly text-based reasoning, but often fail to capture nuanced prosodic cues in speech emotion recognition. Large Audio Language Models, such as Audio Flamingo 3, aim to address this limitation by processing raw au- dio directly. However, these models introduce new challenges related to reasoning stability. In this article, we investigate the impact of prompt- ing strategies (zero-shot, Chain-of-Thought, and multi-turn) on emotion recognition using the MELD and IEMOCAP datasets. We benchmark Audio Flamingo 3 against a modular Whisper-based ASR and LLM pipeline and a multimodal LLM, performing a cost-benefit analysis that considers classification performance and system-level metrics under low- resource constraints. Our results reveal trade-offs between explicit rea- soning and computational efficiency, highlighting the conditions under which structured prompting improves or degrades performance. These findings provide insights for deploying emotion recognition in real-world, resource-constrained environments.
